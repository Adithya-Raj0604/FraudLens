FINTRAC / OSFI JOINT GUIDANCE: Sanctions Screening Requirements
Source: United Nations Act, Criminal Code s.83.08, OSFI Advisory 2022-01
Last Updated: January 2023

LEGAL FRAMEWORK
Canadian financial institutions are subject to sanctions administered by Global Affairs Canada (GAC) under the United Nations Act and the Special Economic Measures Act (SEMA), as well as counter-terrorist property freezing obligations under the Criminal Code. Institutions must not provide financial services — including processing TRANSFER or CASH_OUT transactions — to listed individuals, entities, or jurisdictions.

SCREENING OBLIGATIONS
All customers, beneficial owners, and transaction counterparties must be screened against: the UN Security Council Consolidated List, Canada's Autonomous Sanctions Lists, OFAC SDN List (for USD correspondent relationships), and any jurisdiction-specific lists applicable to the institution's operations. Screening must occur at onboarding, when any party's information changes, and on a rolling basis as lists are updated (typically within 24 hours of a list update).

TRANSACTION MONITORING FOR SANCTIONS
Automated transaction monitoring must flag transactions where: the counterparty name or account identifier matches a listed entity, the destination jurisdiction is subject to comprehensive sanctions (currently: Iran, North Korea, Russia for certain sectors, Syria, Belarus), or the transaction structure is consistent with sanctions evasion (e.g., layering through multiple jurisdictions to obscure origin).

WHEN A MATCH IS DETECTED
Upon a positive sanctions match: (1) freeze all assets immediately; (2) do not process the transaction; (3) report to FINTRAC via STR within 30 days; (4) report to the Royal Canadian Mounted Police (RCMP) if terrorist financing is suspected; (5) notify the institution's Legal and Compliance team within 2 business hours. Staff must not tip off the customer.

RECORDKEEPING
All sanctions screening records, including negative screening results for high-risk transactions, must be retained for 5 years. Screening logic version history must be maintained to demonstrate what list versions were active at the time of each screening event.

PENALTIES
Violations of the United Nations Act carry fines of up to $100,000 and/or imprisonment of up to 5 years. Violations of SEMA carry fines of up to $25,000 per day per violation. Regulatory penalties from OSFI can include capital surcharges and mandatory remediation plans.
