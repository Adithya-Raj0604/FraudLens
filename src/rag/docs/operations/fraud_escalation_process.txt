INTERNAL POLICY: Fraud Alert Escalation Process
Document Owner: Fraud Operations — Head of Financial Crime
Effective Date: January 2024 | Review Date: January 2025

PURPOSE
This document defines the mandatory escalation process for fraud alerts generated by the ML-based fraud detection system. It applies to all TRANSFER and CASH_OUT transaction alerts where the model risk score meets or exceeds the decision threshold (currently 0.9828).

TIER 1 — AUTOMATIC HOLD (risk_score >= 0.9828)
All transactions flagged by the ML model at or above the threshold are automatically placed on a temporary hold pending analyst review. The originating customer must not be notified of the hold until the analyst has completed their initial assessment. The hold window is a maximum of 4 business hours before a decision must be made.

TIER 1 ANALYST CHECKLIST
Within 4 hours of alert generation, the assigned Level 1 Fraud Analyst must:
1. Review the ML risk score and SHAP attribution — identify the top 3 features driving the score.
2. Check if newbalanceOrig = $0.00 (account fully drained — high fraud indicator).
3. Check if oldbalanceDest = $0.00 (receiving account was empty before this transfer — mule account indicator).
4. Review velocity counters: velocity_1hr, velocity_3hr, velocity_24hr — flag if any exceed internal benchmarks (>3, >5, >10 respectively).
5. Pull the account's 90-day transaction history and look for prior similar patterns.
6. Cross-reference the destination account against the mule account watchlist.
7. Decision: Clear, Escalate to Tier 2, or File STR.

TIER 2 — SENIOR ANALYST REVIEW (Complex cases or Tier 1 inconclusive)
Tier 2 reviews are conducted by a Senior Fraud Analyst or the Team Lead. Tier 2 is mandatory when: the risk_score >= 0.9828 AND amount > $50,000 CAD; or the account has had 3 or more prior Tier 1 reviews within 90 days; or the SHAP top driver is "Origin balance (before)" with a value > 0.15 SHAP units; or the transaction involves a PEP-linked account.

TIER 3 — FINANCIAL CRIMES ESCALATION (STR Candidates)
Tier 3 escalation is mandatory when the Tier 2 analyst cannot clear the transaction and any of the following are present: amount > $100,000 CAD with no business justification; velocity_24hr > 10; origin and destination accounts both show $0.00 post-transaction; or the destination account was opened within the past 30 days. The DCO must be notified, and the STR must be filed within 3 business days.

DECISION AUDIT TRAIL
Every decision at each tier — including the analyst's name, timestamp, rationale, and the ML model output at the time of review — must be logged in the case management system. This log is retained for 7 years and is subject to FINTRAC examination.

PERFORMANCE STANDARDS
Tier 1 resolution target: 95% of alerts resolved within 4 business hours. Tier 2 resolution target: 90% within 1 business day. STR filing target: 100% within 30 calendar days of initial alert. Monthly metrics are reported to the Chief Risk Officer and the Board Risk Committee.
