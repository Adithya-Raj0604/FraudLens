INTERNAL POLICY: Account Opening Controls and Mule Account Prevention
Document Owner: Retail Banking Operations / Financial Crime
Effective Date: April 2024 | Review Date: April 2025

PURPOSE
This policy establishes controls to prevent fraudulent account opening, with particular focus on preventing the establishment of mule accounts used to receive and rapidly transfer stolen funds.

IDENTITY VERIFICATION REQUIREMENTS AT ONBOARDING
All new account applicants must provide: a valid government-issued photo ID, proof of address dated within 90 days, and a valid Social Insurance Number (SIN) or Business Number (BN) for entities. Digital onboarding must use a combination of document verification (AI-assisted liveness check) and credit bureau validation. Applications that fail liveness checks or produce no credit file match are referred to a human reviewer within 24 hours.

MULE ACCOUNT DETECTION AT ONBOARDING
The following application patterns must be referred to the Financial Crime team before account approval: (1) Applicant's address was used to open more than 2 accounts in the past 12 months (different applicants); (2) Email address domain was registered within the past 30 days; (3) IP address associated with the application is a known VPN exit node or Tor exit node; (4) Device fingerprint matches a device used in a prior fraud investigation; (5) Application submitted within 1 hour of a rejected application from a different identity at the same IP.

EARLY-LIFE MONITORING
All accounts in the first 90 days of existence are subject to enhanced transaction monitoring with 50% lower alert thresholds. Specifically: TRANSFER or CASH_OUT transactions > $5,000 CAD within the first 30 days require analyst review; velocity_1hr > 2 within the first 30 days triggers an automatic hold; any transaction that reduces the origin balance to $0.00 in the first 30 days is held pending review.

MULE ACCOUNT WATCHLIST
The Financial Crime team maintains a mule account watchlist populated by: confirmed mule accounts from internal investigations, accounts shared by law enforcement under a Production Order, accounts from industry information-sharing networks (Interac, CBA). Inbound transfers to watchlisted destination accounts are automatically blocked and escalated.

ACCOUNT RESTRICTIONS PENDING REVIEW
During a fraud review, the following restrictions are applied automatically: outbound TRANSFER limit reduced to $500/day; CASH_OUT disabled; international wire transfers blocked. Restrictions are lifted only upon documented clearance by a Tier 2 Fraud Analyst or above.
