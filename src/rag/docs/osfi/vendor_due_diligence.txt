OSFI GUIDANCE: Third-Party Risk Management
Source: OSFI Guideline B-10, Third-Party Risk Management
Last Updated: September 2023

SCOPE
OSFI Guideline B-10 applies to all material third-party arrangements entered into by federally regulated financial institutions (FRFIs), including arrangements with technology vendors providing fraud detection software, cloud infrastructure, AI/ML platform services, and regulatory data providers.

MATERIALITY ASSESSMENT
An arrangement is material if its failure or disruption would have a significant impact on the institution's operations, reputation, financial condition, or compliance obligations. All fraud detection and transaction monitoring systems are presumed material given their role in AML/ATF compliance.

DUE DILIGENCE BEFORE ENGAGEMENT
Before entering a material arrangement, FRFIs must: (1) assess the vendor's financial health and operational resilience; (2) review the vendor's information security program and SOC 2 Type II reports; (3) evaluate data residency — all transaction data used in fraud scoring must remain within Canada or jurisdictions with equivalent privacy protections; (4) confirm the vendor's AML compliance posture; (5) obtain references from other financial institution clients.

CONTRACTUAL REQUIREMENTS FOR MATERIAL VENDORS
Contracts must include: right to audit and inspect clauses, data ownership and return/destruction provisions, incident notification timelines (vendor must notify institution within 4 hours of a data breach), sub-contractor disclosure requirements, and SLAs with financial penalties for uptime failures affecting fraud monitoring.

ONGOING MONITORING
FRFIs must conduct annual reviews of material vendors. Reviews must assess: any changes to the vendor's control environment, open audit findings, security incidents since last review, and any changes to the sub-contractor chain. A risk rating must be assigned (High/Medium/Low) and documented.

CONCENTRATION RISK
Institutions must assess and manage concentration risk arising from reliance on a small number of technology vendors for critical functions. Where a single vendor provides both the ML scoring infrastructure and the transaction monitoring alert system, this concentration must be disclosed in the Technology Risk Report to the board.

EXIT AND SUBSTITUTION PLANNING
For each material arrangement, the institution must maintain a documented exit plan that includes: a timeline for transitioning to an alternative provider, data migration procedures, and a tested fallback to manual review processes. Exit plans must be tested at minimum every 3 years.
